Research

Analysis and Diagnostic Interpretation of Therapeutic Factor VIII Plasmid in Hemophilia A Using Logistic and Exponential Regressions

An overview: Hemophilia A is a genetic disorder caused by a deficiency or defect in factor VIII protein, affecting every 1 in 5,000 male births and leading to improper blood coagulation. FVIII plasmid injections (Hemophilia A gene therapy), is used to replace the defective protein and prevent spontaneous bleeding. Analyzing the effects of FVIII plasmid injection on Anti-FVIII antibodies and FVIII activities is vital for optimizing patient care. Through regression analysis the FVIII activity following a negative exponential formula, while the Anti-FVIII antibodies follow a logistic growth pattern compared to days after injection. The analysis provides the optimal timing for re-administration of FVIII plasmid injections, helping 200,000 patients worldwide.
